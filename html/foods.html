Traditional Foods

Takla (Asida)

Traditional porridge made from corn or barley flour

Traditional porridge made from corn or barley flour, commonly served during special occasions like the Amazigh New Year (January).

Berkukes

Large couscous-like pasta served with broth and vegetables

A traditional dish made with hand-rolled pasta similar to couscous but larger in size, cooked with broth and vegetables.

Badaz

Corn couscous, a traditional variation of regular couscous

A traditional couscous made with corn flour instead of wheat, prepared in the same way as regular couscous.

Ourkimen

Traditional Atlas soup made with barley or wheat

A famous Atlas soup made with barley or wheat, vegetables, and traditional spices.

Toumint (Zamita)

Roasted barley flour mixed with water, milk, or oil

Roasted barley flour mixed with water, milk, or oil to create a nutritious and quick meal.

Lebsis

Barley or wheat dish prepared with milk or buttermilk

A nutritious and simple dish made from barley or wheat, prepared with milk or buttermilk.

Lemris

Traditional soup that varies by region

A traditional soup that varies by region, made with buttermilk or butter and various ingredients.

Tafernut

Traditional round Amazigh bread baked in clay ovens

Traditional round Amazigh bread baked in clay ovens, typically served with argan oil or natural butter. This bread is a staple of Amazigh cuisine and is known for its unique texture and flavor.

Taqsha (Bissara)

Traditional fava bean soup made with dried and mashed fava beans

Traditional fava bean soup made with dried and mashed fava beans, olive oil, and spices.
